Replace a Laptop HDD with an SSD

Speed up boot and app loads by 3‚Äì5√ó by upgrading to a 2.5" SATA SSD.

Before you start

  • Back up important files to cloud or USB drive.
  • Have a small Phillips screwdriver and plastic pry tool.
  • Power off the laptop and unplug the charger.

Steps

  1. Remove bottom cover: unscrew all screws (some are under rubber feet). Pry gently along the edges.
  2. Disconnect the battery (if accessible) to avoid shorts.
  3. Locate the 2.5" drive bay. Unscrew the drive caddy and disconnect the SATA cable.
  4. Swap in the SSD, reattach the cable, secure with screws, and reassemble.
  5. Boot and enter BIOS to confirm the SSD is detected.
  6. Install Windows (see Clean Install Windows) or clone old drive to the SSD using a USB‚ÄìSATA adapter.

Add RAM to a Laptop

Reduce stutter and speed up multitasking by adding more memory.

  2. Insert the SODIMM at a ~30¬∞ angle, gold pins first, then press down until side clips click.

Clean Laptop Fans & Vents

Lower temperatures and noise for better performance and battery life.

  1. Power off, unplug, and open the back cover.
  2. Hold the fan blades with a plastic stick and blow short bursts of compressed air.
  3. Brush dust from heatsinks and vents. Avoid spinning fans with air blasts.
  4. Reassemble and monitor temps with HWInfo or similar.
